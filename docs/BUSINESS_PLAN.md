# ArtistPass Business Plan

Last updated: 2026-07-08

This is a practical business plan for turning ArtistPass into a small, revenue-generating productized service and open-source template. It is not yet a venture-scale SaaS plan. The correct first move is proof through paid setups, partners and repeatable delivery.

## Executive Summary

ArtistPass is a self-managed actor and artist portfolio website template with showreels, headshots, casting cards, resume/CV, share links and browser Admin publishing.

The wedge:

- Actors, singers, models and performers need a professional online profile.
- They already use headshots, resumes, reels and contact links.
- Existing builders are either generic DIY tools, low-cost freelancer gigs or actor-specific SaaS platforms.
- ArtistPass can win a narrow slice by being fast, polished, self-editable and service-assisted.

The first business model should be:

1. Free open-source template for trust, search and developer adoption.
2. Paid assisted setup for non-technical artists.
3. Repeatable partner sales through photographers, acting coaches, reel editors and talent managers.
4. Maintenance/help plans after setup.

Do not build full SaaS accounts, billing or private video hosting until the service proves paid demand.

## Target Customer

Primary paying customer:

- Actor, performer, singer, model, anchor or creator who has photos and links but no polished website.

Better first buyer:

- Photographer, acting coach, reel editor, casting workshop, small talent manager or creative agency that can repeat the same setup for multiple artists.

Why partners first:

- Individual artists may be price-sensitive and inconsistent.
- Partners already sell adjacent services.
- One partner can produce multiple sites.
- The website becomes an add-on to an existing portfolio, reel or training package.

## Product Offer

Core product:

- One-page cinematic portfolio website.
- Hero profile.
- Role-fit or persona cards.
- Showreel/reel section.
- Headshot/gallery section.
- Casting card or EPK card.
- Resume/CV PDF.
- Native share flows.
- Browser Admin for edits, uploads and live publish.
- Optional custom domain guidance.

What is not included yet:

- Full SaaS accounts.
- Private video hosting.
- Payment processing.
- Talent CRM.
- Casting director database.
- Analytics beyond basic website analytics.

## Pricing

Customer pays separately for:

- Domain name.
- Vercel account if they choose paid hosting.
- Third-party video hosting.
- Professional photos, reels or design assets.

## Funnel Assumptions

These are operating assumptions, not market facts. They must be tracked weekly.

Direct outreach:

- 200 targeted messages/month.
- 10% reply rate = 20 replies.
- 40% of replies become calls = 8 calls.
- 25% of calls close = 2 paid customers.

Partner outreach:

- 20 partner targets/month.
- 20% conversation rate = 4 conversations.
- 25% become trial partners = 1 partner.
- Each active partner should produce 1-3 projects/month after trust is built.

Marketplace:

- Slow start.
- Target 1 paid project/month by month 3.

Content:

- Slow compounding channel.
- Measure profile visits, GitHub visits, demo visits and inquiries.

## Capacity Model

Estimated delivery time:

- Launch: 4-6 hours.
- Pro: 8-10 hours.
- Premium: 16-20 hours.
- Maintenance client: 0.5-1 hour/month.

Base case month 12 capacity load:

- 5 Launch projects = about 25 hours.
- 5 Pro projects = about 45 hours.
- 2 Premium projects = about 36 hours.
- 20 maintenance clients = about 10-20 hours.
- Total = about 116-126 hours/month.

This is possible for one focused operator, but tight. If base case is reached, outsource asset cleanup, copy formatting and QA before building more product features.

## Costs And Margin

Direct cash costs should stay low.

| Cost | Assumption | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hosting | Customer owns Vercel project where possible. | Avoid hosting liability. |
| Vercel | Hobby is USD 0/month; Pro is USD 20/month. | Use Pro only when needed for a client or our own product. |
| Blob/storage | Included on Hobby within limits; usage pricing applies beyond included limits. | Keep images compressed and videos link-based. |
| Domain | Customer pays. | We guide setup. |
| Payment fees | Assume 2-3%. | Depends on payment method. |
| Tools | INR 0-5,000/month initially. | Do not add SaaS tools until revenue exists. |
| Paid ads | INR 0 initially. | Use only after conversion is proven. |

Gross margin should be high because this is service plus reusable software. The real constraint is founder/operator time.

## Go/No-Go Gates

30-day gate:

- At least 2 paid pilots, or 5 serious calls with clear objections.

60-day gate:

- At least 5 total paid customers.
- At least 1 partner willing to refer.
- Delivery time under 10 hours for Launch/Pro average.

90-day gate:

- At least INR 2,00,000 cumulative revenue.
- At least 2 repeatable lead sources.
- At least 10 maintenance clients or clear evidence customers will self-manage.

If these gates fail:

- Keep ArtistPass as an open-source reputation project.
- Do not build SaaS.
- Use it as a portfolio case study and reuse the architecture for another vertical.

If these gates pass:

- Productize onboarding.
- Add more vertical templates.
- Consider simple paid template/support layer.
- Only then consider SaaS accounts and billing.

## Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Artists want links, not websites. | Low conversion. | Sell to partners and serious performers; position as share-ready profile, not vanity site. |
| Cheap freelancer competition. | Price pressure. | Use stronger design, speed, Admin handover and casting-specific share flows. |
| Too much custom work. | Low margin. | Fixed packages, strict asset checklist, limited revisions. |
| User cannot manage Vercel/GitHub. | Support burden. | Assisted setup, customer-owned project, simple Admin handover. |
| Media privacy expectations. | Product mismatch. | Keep videos link-based and clearly explain privacy limits. |
| Legal/consent risk with real identities. | Serious risk. | Use written approval for public samples; use fictional samples by default. |
| Founder gets pulled into endless updates. | Margin collapse. | Maintenance plan or self-admin only. No free forever support. |

## Decision

Proceed as a 90-day productized service experiment.

Do not build more platform complexity until paid demand is proven.

The immediate objective is not "become SaaS." The immediate objective is:

> 10 paid portfolio setups, 2 referral partners and INR 2,00,000+ revenue within 90 days.
